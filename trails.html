Lake Eola Loop is located in Dowtown Orlando. It is roughly a 20 minute walk from UCF's Downtown Campus. This 0.9 mile paved trail features aesthetics both natural and urban, making it an ideal starting point for first-time grass-touchers.


History

Origins

In 1873, Jacob Summerlin purchased 200 acres of land where a sinkhole filled with water from an aquifer formed Lake Eola.

The Summerlin family donated land around the lake in 1883, naming it after Robert Summerlin's late sweetheart, which then became a public park in 1888.

Swans

Swans were first introduced in 1922 and now include several species such as Trumpeter, Black Neck, and Austrailian Black Sawns. They park conducts an annual Swan Round-up for veterinary care vaccination.

Landmarks

  • The Eola House (1924): A Mediterranean Revival-style home serving as park offices and event space.
  • The Chinese Ting: A friendship gift from Nelson Ying, originally built in Shanghai.
  • Battle of the Bulge Monument (1999): Honors World War II veterans.
  • Walt Disney Amphitheater: Originally built in 1886, reconstructed on the lake’s west side in 1989.
  • Japanese Rock Garden: Features a 12.5-ton black marble from Tainan, Taiwan, symbolizing Orlando’s sister city friendship.
  • Sperry Fountain (1914): Donated by Mayor E. Frank Sperry, featuring wrought iron duck and leaf designs.
  • Linton E. Allen Memorial Fountain (1957): The park’s centerpiece, known for its water displays and lighting. After a lightning strike in 2009, it was restored in 2011, with original green glass preserved in the Eola House.

Flora

Common plant life includes large live oaks, palm tree, and cypress trees contributing to the park's tropical and shaded landscape.

Fauna

Lake Eola is home to a variety of wildlife, most notably several non-native swan pieces originally transferred from Lake Lucerne due to territorial conflicts. These include:

  • Whooper Swans: White with yellow beaks extending past the nostrils; native to Europe and Asia Minor.
  • Black Neck Swans: Black-necked and mostly water-bound; from South America.
  • Australian Black Swans: Black-feathered swans native to Australia.
  • Royal Mute Swans: Less vocal, white with orange and black bills and a black knob; from Europe and Asia.
  • Trumpeter Swans: Native to North America, white with black beaks.

Other animals found around the park include rock pigeons, Muscovy ducks, wading birds (ibis, herons, egrets), Egyptian geese, pond turtles, and eastern gray squirrels.

Conservation

Fishing is prohibited, and numerous trash cans encourage cleanliness. Visitors are reminded to protect wildlife, such as using designated feed dispensers for birds, since other foods can make them sick.

The Arboretum is located on the east side of the UCF campus. There are many different trails featured in The Arboretum, but we recommend Wildflower Loop, which is only 1.2 miles long. This trail is designated by orange path markers. You do not have to be a UCF student to visit here.


Flora

Wildflowers

  • Blazing Star
  • Coastalplain Honeycombhead
  • Florida Paintbrush
  • Florida Tickseed
  • Forked Bluecurls
  • Fourpetal St. John's Wort
  • Goldenrod
  • Rayless Sunflower
  • Scarlet Calamint
  • Shortleaf Rosegentian
  • Titusville Balm
  • Waterlily

Trees

  • Longleaf Pine
  • Pond Cypress
  • Sabal Palm
  • Sand Live Oak
  • Wax Myrtle

Fauna

The animals here are shyer than in other trails like Lake Eola loop, but there are still plenty of creatures that call this place home such as

  • Bobcats
  • Bobwhite Quails
  • Coyotes
  • Gopher Tortoises
  • Great Blue Heron
  • Gray Foxes
  • Gray Squirrels
  • Raccoons
  • White-Tailed Deer

Conservation

UCF Land managers use prescribed fires to help maintain the ecosystem and prevent larger, unplanned fires. Be mindful of wildlife for both the safety of you and them. If you come across an injured animal, call a professional.

While you can explore the area yourself at your own pace, The Arboretum does offer two different hour-long guided tours for only $5 per person, per tour.

The Pavilion can be rented out and includes a grill.

Outside of the Wildflower Loop, The Arboretum has many interesting facilities and features that are worth checking out

  • Apiary
  • Boardwalk
  • Community Garden
  • The Greenhouse
